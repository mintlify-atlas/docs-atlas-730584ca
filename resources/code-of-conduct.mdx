The Kanagawa project has adopted the Microsoft Open Source Code of Conduct to ensure a welcoming and inclusive environment for all contributors and community members.

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for everyone. This Code of Conduct outlines our expectations for participant behavior as well as the consequences for unacceptable behavior.

<Note>
  All participants in the Kanagawa community are expected to abide by this Code of Conduct in all community venues—online and in-person—as well as in all one-on-one communications.
</Note>

## Microsoft Open Source Code of Conduct

This project has adopted the [Microsoft Open Source Code of Conduct](https://opensource.microsoft.com/codeofconduct/).

### Expected Behavior

All community members are expected to:

<Check>Be respectful and considerate in all interactions</Check>
<Check>Use welcoming and inclusive language</Check>
<Check>Be collaborative and constructive</Check>
<Check>Focus on what is best for the community</Check>
<Check>Show empathy towards other community members</Check>
<Check>Accept constructive criticism gracefully</Check>
<Check>Respect differing viewpoints and experiences</Check>

### Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered harassment and are unacceptable:

<Warning>Violent threats or language directed against another person</Warning>
<Warning>Discriminatory jokes and language</Warning>
<Warning>Posting sexually explicit or violent material</Warning>
<Warning>Personal insults or attacks</Warning>
<Warning>Trolling or insulting/derogatory comments</Warning>
<Warning>Public or private harassment</Warning>
<Warning>Publishing others' private information without explicit permission</Warning>
<Warning>Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting</Warning>

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- **GitHub repositories** - Issues, pull requests, discussions, and comments
- **Project communication channels** - Any official Kanagawa communication platforms
- **Community events** - Virtual or in-person events related to the project
- **Representation** - When representing the project or community

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.

Project maintainers have the right and responsibility to:
- Remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct
- Ban temporarily or permanently any contributor for behaviors that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful

## Consequences

Participants who are asked to stop any unacceptable behavior are expected to comply immediately.

If a community member engages in unacceptable behavior, the project maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate, including:

1. **Warning** - A private, written warning providing clarity around the nature of the violation
2. **Temporary Ban** - A temporary ban from interacting with the community
3. **Permanent Ban** - A permanent ban from the community
